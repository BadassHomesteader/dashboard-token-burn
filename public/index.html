How to Read This

Heuristics for a fifteen-minute weekly review (our interpretation).
  • A quiet stretch can be worse than a spike. Falling burn often means you stopped delegating, not that you got efficient.
  • Watch the computer-vs-assistant split. Rising computer-work share means more transformative delegation, not just chat.
  • Read the trend, not the day. Use the 30-day average; single days are noisy.
  • Check what drove it. A spike from coding is different from a spike from one runaway session.
  • Cache is most of the volume. Big numbers are mostly cached context reads — burn ≠ cost.
